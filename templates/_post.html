The Day in Sunlight 7/2/2010

Posted on
July 2, 2010
Time
9:52 a.m.
by
Eric Naing

Here is a look at what’s been happening on the Sunlight network.

  • Google and Microsoft, both supporters of net neutrality, have hired 112 former government officials to lobby Congress. (Sunlight Foundation Blog)
  • Computer scientist Matt Chung announced his new Phoenix-area open government group PhxData. (Sunlight Labs)
  • A year after Data.gov launched, Gabriel Debenedetti looked at what’s good and what needs improving. (Sunlight Foundation Reporting Group)
  • Traver Riggins discussed RECAP, a Firefox plug-in that grants free access to many federal court documents. (Sunlight Foundation Reporting Group)
  • Ryan Sibley looked at the state of North Carolina’s response to the Citizens United ruling.(Sunlight Foundation Reporting Group)
  • Using the augmented reality app LayAR, you can now check out Party Time fundraisers on your smartphone. (Party Time)
